How to Get From MCO to Disney World: The Best Options for Families

Touching down at Orlando International Airport (MCO) is the start of a magical vacation, but it can also be the start of a logistical headache. You have navigated the 9th busiest airport in the U.S., grabbed your luggage, and wrangled the kids. Now, you just want to get to your resort.

Since the free Disney Magical Express service ended in 2022, families have to find their own way to the Mouse. The good news? You have plenty of choices. The bad news? Not all of them are created equal.

We are local experts who drive these roads every day. We know which routes get backed up on I-4 and which services offer the best value for families. This guide breaks down exactly how to get from the airport to your hotel so you can start your vacation stress-free.

What are your main MCO airport transportation options?

When looking for MCO airport transportation, you generally have four main categories to choose from. Each serves a different type of traveler.

  • Shuttle Buses (Mears Connect): Large buses that transport multiple families to various resorts.
  • Ride-Sharing (Uber/Lyft): App-based services that pick you up curbside.
  • Rental Cars: Driving yourself to the resort.
  • Private Car Service/Taxis: Direct transport reserved specifically for your group.

Understanding the difference between these can save you both money and precious vacation time.

How do these options compare on cost and convenience?

The "best" option depends entirely on your priorities. Are you trying to save every penny, or is your goal to get to the pool as fast as possible?

Shuttle Buses: These are generally the cheapest option for solo travelers or couples, costing around $16 per adult one way. However, you pay per person. For a family of four or five, the cost quickly rivals a private car. The downside? You wait for the bus to fill up, and you may make several stops at other hotels before reaching yours.

Ride-Sharing: Uber and Lyft are often faster than shuttles. The cost varies wildly based on demand (surge pricing). While convenient for adults, they are difficult for families with young children because standard rides do not provide car seats.

Private Car Service: This is the gold standard for convenience. You have a driver meeting you at baggage claim, helping with luggage, and driving you directly to your resort in a luxury SUV.

Taxis: You can still hail a taxi from MCO to Disney World right at the curb. It is a straightforward option, but often more expensive than pre-arranged services, and the vehicles vary in quality.

Is ride-sharing a reliable choice for families?

Apps like Uber and Lyft are fantastic for quick trips around town, or even for Universal Orlando resort transportation if you plan to hop between parks. However, for airport transfers with kids, they can be tricky.

Surge Pricing:
Flight arrivals often happen in waves. When hundreds of people land at once, demand spikes. A ride that normally costs $40 could suddenly jump to $80 or more.

The Car Seat Problem:
Most ride-share drivers do not carry car seats. Uber Car Seat is an option, but availability is very limited and usually only provides one forward-facing seat. If you have a toddler and an infant, you're out of luck unless you brought your own seats and are willing to install them on the curb while traffic rushes by.

Why might you want (or want to avoid) a rental car?

Renting a car gives you total freedom. You can drive to the beaches on the East Coast, visit International Drive, or grab groceries at a local Publix.

However, the costs add up fast. You have the daily rental rate, plus gas and tolls on the Beachline Expressway (Highway 528).

Why it matters:
The hidden killer is parking fees. Standard overnight parking at Disney Deluxe Resorts is $25 per night. For a week-long trip, you're looking at an extra $175 just to park a car you might not use often if you're staying in the "Disney Bubble."

Is the Mears Connect shuttle easy to use?

Mears Connect is the spiritual successor to the Magical Express. It is located in Terminal B, Level 1.

It is a reliable service, but it lacks the speed of a private transfer. You check in, wait in line for your bus, wait for the bus to load, and then sit through the route. If your hotel is the last stop, your travel time could double. If you're eager to start your vacation immediately, the bus might feel like a slow start.

Is a taxi from MCO to Disney World cost-effective?

For some travelers, walking out to the curb and hopping in a taxi from MCO to Disney World feels like the easiest move. There is no app to download and no booking required.

However, traditional taxis run on a meter. If there is an accident on I-4 or heavy congestion near Lake Buena Vista (which is common), that meter keeps running. You also miss out on the perks of private service, like grocery stops or guaranteed car seats. While a taxi from MCO to Disney World gets the job done, you often pay premium prices for a non-premium experience.
